1. Understand AI security concepts and terminology

## Book Structure

### Part 1: Introduction to AI Security

**1. Overview of Cybersecurity for AI Systems**  
- AI & Cybersecurity intersection  
- Unique AI vulnerabilities: data poisoning, model evasion  
- Skills: Identify AI threats, assess impacts, apply mitigation  

**2. Protecting AI Models and Algorithms**  
- Threats: model inversion, IP theft  
- Defense: encryption, diff privacy, model sharing  
- Skills: Secure models, algorithms, tamper-proofing  
- **Lab**: Identify vulnerabilities in AI systems

**3. Regulatory and Compliance in AI Security**  
- Apply GDPR, NIST, ISO/IEC to AI  
- Secure data pipelines, prevent data leakage  
- Skills: Governance, end-to-end pipeline security, compliance

---

### Part 2: Securing AI Data and Pipelines

**4. Data Security in AI Systems**  
- Secure training & inference data  
- Techniques: encryption, anonymization  
- Skills: Data protection at rest/in transit, access controls

**5. Pipeline Security in AI Systems**  
- Secure pipeline from ingestion to deployment  
- Techniques: encryption, monitoring  
- Skills: Prevent tampering, enforce access, vulnerability scans

**6. Adversarial Attacks on AI Systems**  
- Attacks: evasion, poisoning  
- Defense: adversarial training, preprocessing  
- Skills: Analyze attacks, develop defenses  
- **Lab**: Defend against adversarial examples

**7. AI Model Explainability and Security**  
- Tools: SHAP, LIME  
- Risks of black-box models  
- Skills: Increase transparency, ensure accountability

---

### Part 3: AI Security Architecture and Operations

**8. Cybersecurity Frameworks for AI Development**  
- Secure development lifecycle  
- Secure coding, validation, testing (static/dynamic/adversarial)  
- Skills: Integrate security from start to finish

**9. Monitoring and Incident Response**  
- Continuous AI monitoring  
- Alerts, anomaly detection  
- Skills: Create response plans, secure model deployments  
- **Lab**: Set up secure deployment with telemetry

**10. Zero Trust Security for AI Systems**  
- Zero Trust principles for AI  
- Least privilege, MFA, anomaly detection  
- Skills: Secure architecture, enforce access policies

---

### Part 4: AI Adversarial Attacks & Cloud Cybersecurity

**11. Advanced Adversarial Attacks**  
- Techniques: gradient-based, model inversion, backdoor  
- Defenses: ensemble models, robustness  
- Skills: Simulate and defend against complex attacks

**12. Cloud Security for AI Systems**  
- Secure cloud deployments  
- Access control, encryption, compliance  
- Skills: Protect workloads, monitor cloud AI  
- **Lab**: Simulate cloud adversarial attacks

**13. Ethical Considerations in AI Security**  
- Topics: privacy, fairness, transparency  
- Skills: Detect bias, ensure fairness, follow ethical frameworks
